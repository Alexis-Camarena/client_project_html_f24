Student Well-Being

Physical Health

Maintaining your physical health is essential for academic success and overall well-being. The University of Michigan offers a variety of resources to support your physical health:

University Health Service (UHS)

UHS provides comprehensive medical care, including routine check-ups, vaccinations, specialist referrals, and health education. Learn more about the services they offer by visiting the UHS website.

Recreational Sports

Stay active through a variety of fitness programs, intramural sports, and access to gym facilities. Check out available activities and membership options on the Recreational Sports website.

Nutrition Resources

Find nutritional guidance and meal planning support through campus dietitians. Learn more about healthy eating habits at the Nutrition Services page.

Mental Health

Your mental well-being is crucial for thriving in graduate school. Explore these resources to support your mental health:

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS)

CAPS offers individual counseling, group therapy, workshops, and crisis intervention services. For more information or to schedule an appointment, visit the CAPS website.

Wellness Coaching

Work with a wellness coach to develop strategies for managing stress, improving time management, and enhancing your overall well-being. Learn more about the program at the Wellness Coaching page.

Mindfulness and Meditation

Participate in mindfulness and meditation sessions to cultivate a calm and focused mind. Explore upcoming sessions and resources on the Mindfulness Resources page.

Support Groups

Join peer support groups to connect with others who share similar experiences and challenges. Explore the different groups available through the Support Groups page.

Additional Resources

Explore other well-being resources available at UM:

  • Student Life Well-Being - A hub for all things well-being, including resources on financial wellness, sleep, and community connections.
  • Life in Ann Arbor - Discover the vibrant community of Ann Arbor and find ways to balance life outside of your academic responsibilities.
  • Emergency Services - Immediate help for urgent mental and physical health needs.
